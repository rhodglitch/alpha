Overview

Purpose

The purpose of this website is to let the people know about my white water rafting company. Help rafters navigate to the sections that will best help them, and make them excited for their tour. The navigators who are looking for recreational outdoor activities which use an inflatable raft to navigate a river or other body of water. Teamwork is often a part of the experience. While on vacation they will enjoy to the fullest. Die-hard water rafters will be challenged in their stay. They will be dealing with risk, but my website would teach them to be safe always.

Audience

I want to target young and adults who will explore the outside adventures. The families, different groups, and students. If they like to raft, I want them. It's not just any ordinary rafting trip. I want this trip to be the most exciting trip they ever have. I whould show to them that we offer the best, and most affordable rafting company. They can access my site by any source of technology. There will be a lot of ads on social media that people can click on. We want people to feel like they can come to our site, and feel comfortable as they browse.

Typography

I chose the Lato+QingKe+HuangYou; The reason I chose this font because I think it's still profession and at the same time it's fun! It is important to be consitent with a website, so that's why I'm choosing the one font.

Heading Font: Lobster;

The reason I chose this font as the heading is because it's a little bold, curly, flashy and fun, which will grab the audience attention. First impression last, the heading must be catchy to all who see the heading.

Paragraph Font: Lato+QingKe+HuangYou;

The reason I chose this font because It is easy to read, professional, and it's fun!

Site Plan
 
 
 

The Site Map of a site is just like it sounds…it is a map of the pages in a site and how they are related and linked together. From the map above we can see that we will eventually have the Home page and 2 sub or child pages.

The lines that connect them all together indicate that each page should be accessible from any other page, it is essentially showing us the global navigation for the site.

Wireframes

Wireframes are like blueprints for making webpages. They should show the major sections of content that will be on the page and the relative locations of each element. In the wireframe below you can see there will be 6 sections to our page:

  1. At the top we have a section with the logo (the box with the mountain means an image) and the navigation bar.
  2. Then there is a banner image that stretches all the way across the screen.
  3. Next we have some text and an image
  4. ...followed by another row made up of an image and some text.
  5. Then one more section of text with no image.
  6. Lastly, a footer containing a copyright/name line and 3 social media icons.
